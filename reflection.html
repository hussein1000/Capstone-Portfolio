What I learned from the course and how I plan to grow as a communicator.

Key takeaways

  • Audience-first writing makes documentation usable — always identify the reader and their goals.
  • Structure reduces friction: short steps, clear headings, and visible examples help readers complete tasks.
  • Revision matters: iterative testing with real users exposed assumptions and improved clarity.

Impact on my work

I now prototype documentation early — pairing quickstart guides and task flows with code. I use plain-language summaries at the top of every page to orient readers.

Next steps

  1. Collect user feedback on one manual page and measure task completion.
  2. Iterate on the README to reduce time-to-first-success for new contributors.
  3. Develop a style guide for future docs in the repo (consistent voice & tone).
